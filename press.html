Dave Bakke: I dig songs about Chandlerville

Dave Bakke · State Journal-Register · Published May 26, 2013

Chandlerville is one of my favorite places to fish for column ideas. It remains the only town that ever named me grand marshal of a parade. It is near some of the best duck hunting in Illinois. Those people "get" what I'm looking for in a column. For these reasons, I have an interest in all things Chandlerville.

So when I heard that Jobe Shores had written a song about Chandlerville, well, it got my attention. I don't write this to promote any particular band. It's just that it's rare to hear a song about Chandlerville.

His song is "Sunrise in Chandlerville." He posted a YouTube video of himself playing guitar and performing the song in the woods with Drew Cox of Virginia on bongo drums and Joseph Gardner from Ashland raking (that's right, raking) in the background.

When Drew and Jobe perform in Chandlerville and around Cass County, they are billed as Gumtown Hall. That also is a Chandlerville reference.

"Gumtown, I guess, was a little Sangamon River town," says Jobe. "My grandpa and grandma lived on Gumtown Road (west of Chandlerville). My grandma still lives there, and I stay with her as often as I can. The 'Hall' part is that Drew lives on Hall Road. We came up with a whole bunch of band names and got on YouTube, and they were all taken. So I said we're going to have to combine the two."

So Gumtown Hall it is.

The song "Sunrise in Chandlerville" is about driving around the countryside all night after the bars close, just "staring down the moon/scaring up the sun" until dawn. And "When the sun comes up on Chandlerville/You best call it a night."

Jobe has written another Chandlerville-related song. That one is called "Old Man In The Woods." It's about a legendary old guy who lived most of his life in a shack up in the hills east of Chandlerville.

The video for that one was also recorded out in the woods. Doing that has its advantages and disadvantages. Jobe describes one of those disadvantages: "The first verse of the song, a gnat flew into my mustache and was stuck there until the end of the song when I finally got him out of there. But I love the woods. I'd rather sit and play outside than get something set up inside. I'd rather have it like that, however it turns out."

Jobe has written a song about his grandma who lives on Gumtown Road. It's about her getting too old to drive and so somebody needs to confiscate her car keys. He sang it for her, but she is hard of hearing so he's not sure she caught all of it. At least she knows it's about her.

Gumtown Hall plays every Thursday night at Duke and Joanie's Tavern in Chandlerville. It's not a fancy place by any means, but, hey, it's a place to perform. Their songs probably play particularly well in Cass and Mason counties. The audience gets the references. They know about Gumtown. They know Jobe's grandma. They know about the man who lived in the hills above town.

Actually, you could say what Jobe is doing is the musical version of what I do.
